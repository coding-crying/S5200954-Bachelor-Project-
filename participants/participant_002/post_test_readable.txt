1. Rather than offer constructive criticism, he chose to _____ his opponent's achievements.
   A) disparage
   B) obfuscate
   C) bolster
   D) eschew
   Answer: _______ (correct: disparage)

2. The monastery's _____ living conditions reflected the monks' commitment to simplicity.
   A) austere
   B) garrulous
   C) precocious
   D) copious
   Answer: _______ (correct: austere)

   A) perfunctory
   B) precocious
   C) circumspect
   D) capricious
   Answer: _______ (correct: perfunctory)

4. The _____ beauty of cherry blossoms makes their brief blooming season even more precious.
   A) ephemeral
   B) copious
   C) cursory
   D) vociferous
   Answer: _______ (correct: ephemeral)

5. The movie's _____ plot failed to engage audiences looking for original storytelling.
   A) hackneyed
   B) intractable
   C) deleterious
   D) conciliatory
   Answer: _______ (correct: hackneyed)

6. The politician tried to _____ the facts to avoid taking responsibility for the scandal.
   A) obfuscate
   B) disparage
   C) capitulate
   D) abrogate
   Answer: _______ (correct: obfuscate)

7. The _____ weather made it impossible to plan outdoor activities with confidence.
   A) capricious
   B) circumspect
   C) intractable
   D) abstruse
   Answer: _______ (correct: capricious)

8. Given the sensitive nature of the negotiations, the diplomat remained _____ in his statements.
   A) circumspect
   B) capricious
   C) intractable
   D) deleterious
   Answer: _______ (correct: circumspect)

9. She tried to _____ her reluctant brother into joining the family vacation.
   A) cajole
   B) admonish
   C) disparage
   D) abrogate
   Answer: _______ (correct: cajole)

10. A _____ glance at the report revealed several obvious errors.
   A) cursory
   B) copious
   C) ephemeral
   D) vociferous
   Answer: _______ (correct: cursory)

11. His refreshing _____ during the interview impressed the hiring committee.
   A) candor
   B) acumen
   C) cacophony
   D) conundrum
   Answer: _______ (correct: candor)

12. The professor's _____ explanation of quantum mechanics left most students confused.
   A) abstruse
   B) perfunctory
   C) deleterious
   D) ephemeral
   Answer: _______ (correct: abstruse)

13. The _____ effects of pollution on marine life became increasingly evident.
   A) deleterious
   B) conciliatory
   C) abstruse
   D) hackneyed
   Answer: _______ (correct: deleterious)

14. The teacher had to _____ the students for their disruptive behavior during the assembly.
   A) admonish
   B) cajole
   C) capitulate
   D) bolster
   Answer: _______ (correct: admonish)

15. The _____ dispute had resisted all attempts at resolution for over a decade.
   A) intractable
   B) abstruse
   C) hackneyed
   D) conciliatory
   Answer: _______ (correct: intractable)

16. The positive reviews helped _____ confidence in the company's new product line.
   A) bolster
   B) obfuscate
   C) eschew
   D) admonish
   Answer: _______ (correct: bolster)

17. The _____ protests outside the courthouse could be heard from several blocks away.
   A) vociferous
   B) copious
   C) cursory
   D) ephemeral
   Answer: _______ (correct: vociferous)

18. The _____ passenger talked non-stop throughout the entire flight.
   A) garrulous
   B) austere
   C) precocious
   D) copious
   Answer: _______ (correct: garrulous)

   A) eschew
   B) bolster
   C) disparage
   D) obfuscate
   Answer: _______ (correct: eschew)

   C) admonish
