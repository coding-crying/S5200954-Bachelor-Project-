PART A: FILL IN THE BLANK
Instructions: Fill in the blank with the correct vocabulary word from your learning session.

WORD BANK: deleterious | hackneyed | obfuscate | garrulous | intractable | despot | ephemeral | bolster | conciliatory | capricious | eschew | disparage | cursory | precocious | copious | candor | cacophony | perfunctory | vociferous | capitulate | abrogate | admonish | circumspect | conundrum

1. The _____ effects of pollution on marine life became increasingly evident.
   Answer: _______ (correct: deleterious)

2. The movie's _____ plot failed to engage audiences looking for original storytelling.
   Answer: _______ (correct: hackneyed)

3. The politician tried to _____ the facts to avoid taking responsibility for the scandal.
   Answer: _______ (correct: obfuscate)

4. The _____ passenger talked non-stop throughout the entire flight.
   Answer: _______ (correct: garrulous)

5. The _____ dispute had resisted all attempts at resolution for over a decade.
   Answer: _______ (correct: intractable)

6. The cruel _____ ruled through fear and intimidation for decades.
   Answer: _______ (correct: despot)

7. The _____ beauty of cherry blossoms makes their brief blooming season even more precious.
   Answer: _______ (correct: ephemeral)

8. The positive reviews helped _____ confidence in the company's new product line.
   Answer: _______ (correct: bolster)

9. After the heated argument, he adopted a _____ tone to restore peace.
   Answer: _______ (correct: conciliatory)

10. The _____ weather made it impossible to plan outdoor activities with confidence.
   Answer: _______ (correct: capricious)

11. Health-conscious consumers increasingly _____ processed foods in favor of natural alternatives.
   Answer: _______ (correct: eschew)

12. Rather than offer constructive criticism, he chose to _____ his opponent's achievements.
   Answer: _______ (correct: disparage)

13. A _____ glance at the report revealed several obvious errors.
   Answer: _______ (correct: cursory)

14. The _____ child was reading university-level texts at age ten.
   Answer: _______ (correct: precocious)

15. The researcher took _____ notes during the lengthy interview session.
   Answer: _______ (correct: copious)

16. His refreshing _____ during the interview impressed the hiring committee.
   Answer: _______ (correct: candor)

17. The _____ of car horns and construction noise made conversation impossible.
   Answer: _______ (correct: cacophony)

18. Her _____ apology lacked sincerity and failed to address the real issues.
   Answer: _______ (correct: perfunctory)

19. The _____ protests outside the courthouse could be heard from several blocks away.
   Answer: _______ (correct: vociferous)

20. After weeks of resistance, the city was forced to _____ to the enemy's demands.
   Answer: _______ (correct: capitulate)

21. The new government decided to _____ the controversial treaty signed by its predecessor.
   Answer: _______ (correct: abrogate)

22. The teacher had to _____ the students for their disruptive behavior during the assembly.
   Answer: _______ (correct: admonish)

23. Given the sensitive nature of the negotiations, the diplomat remained _____ in his statements.
   Answer: _______ (correct: circumspect)

24. The detective faced a perplexing _____ with no obvious solution in sight.
   Answer: _______ (correct: conundrum)
