PART A: MULTIPLE CHOICE
Instructions: Choose the best word to complete each sentence.

1. The politician tried to _____ the facts to avoid taking responsibility for the scandal.
   A) obfuscate
   B) capitulate
   C) abrogate
   D) disparage
   Answer: _______ (correct: obfuscate)

2. The positive reviews helped _____ confidence in the company's new product line.
   A) obfuscate
   B) bolster
   C) eschew
   D) admonish
   Answer: _______ (correct: bolster)

3. The _____ child was reading university-level texts at age ten.
   A) austere
   B) garrulous
   C) precocious
   D) vociferous
   Answer: _______ (correct: precocious)

4. Her exceptional business _____ helped transform the struggling company into a market leader.
   A) cacophony
   B) candor
   C) conundrum
   D) acumen
   Answer: _______ (correct: acumen)

5. She tried to _____ her reluctant brother into joining the family vacation.
   A) cajole
   B) admonish
   C) abrogate
   D) disparage
   Answer: _______ (correct: cajole)

6. After the heated argument, he adopted a _____ tone to restore peace.
   A) conciliatory
   B) deleterious
   C) hackneyed
   D) perfunctory
   Answer: _______ (correct: conciliatory)

7. His refreshing _____ during the interview impressed the hiring committee.
   A) cacophony
   B) candor
   C) conundrum
   D) acumen
   Answer: _______ (correct: candor)

8. A _____ glance at the report revealed several obvious errors.
   A) ephemeral
   B) vociferous
   C) cursory
   D) copious
   Answer: _______ (correct: cursory)

9. The new government decided to _____ the controversial treaty signed by its predecessor.
   A) disparage
   B) bolster
   C) eschew
   D) abrogate
   Answer: _______ (correct: abrogate)

10. Rather than offer constructive criticism, he chose to _____ his opponent's achievements.
   A) obfuscate
   B) bolster
   C) eschew
   D) disparage
   Answer: _______ (correct: disparage)

11. The _____ weather made it impossible to plan outdoor activities with confidence.
   A) circumspect
   B) abstruse
   C) intractable
   D) capricious
   Answer: _______ (correct: capricious)

12. The _____ passenger talked non-stop throughout the entire flight.
   A) garrulous
   B) austere
   C) precocious
   D) copious
   Answer: _______ (correct: garrulous)

13. After weeks of resistance, the city was forced to _____ to the enemy's demands.
   A) capitulate
   B) abrogate
   C) cajole
   D) admonish
   Answer: _______ (correct: capitulate)

14. Faced with two equally unappealing options, she found herself in a difficult _____.
   A) conundrum
   B) acumen
   C) cacophony
   D) quandary
   Answer: _______ (correct: quandary)

15. The teacher had to _____ the students for their disruptive behavior during the assembly.
   A) capitulate
   B) bolster
   C) cajole
   D) admonish
   Answer: _______ (correct: admonish)

16. The _____ of car horns and construction noise made conversation impossible.
   A) acumen
   B) cacophony
   C) candor
   D) quandary
   Answer: _______ (correct: cacophony)

17. Her _____ apology lacked sincerity and failed to address the real issues.
   A) capricious
   B) precocious
   C) perfunctory
   D) circumspect
   Answer: _______ (correct: perfunctory)

18. The _____ beauty of cherry blossoms makes their brief blooming season even more precious.
   A) ephemeral
   B) copious
   C) vociferous
   D) cursory
   Answer: _______ (correct: ephemeral)

19. The _____ effects of pollution on marine life became increasingly evident.
   A) deleterious
   B) conciliatory
   C) hackneyed
   D) abstruse
   Answer: _______ (correct: deleterious)

20. Given the sensitive nature of the negotiations, the diplomat remained _____ in his statements.
   A) intractable
   B) deleterious
   C) circumspect
   D) capricious
   Answer: _______ (correct: circumspect)
